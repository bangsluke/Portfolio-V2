Two weeks into actively pursuing the transition to product, and it's been a busy start. Here's where things stand.

## Progress So Far

The biggest time investment has been completing the <span class="theme-link">Product</span> School Product Strategy Micro-Certification (PSC), covering market analysis, customer analytics, product vision and mission, stakeholder communication, <span class="theme-link">OKRs</span>, roadmaps, and prioritisation. It's a solid structured programme and it's helping me build the vocabulary and frameworks I'll need alongside the practical experience I already have.

On the reading side, I've been continuing with _<span class="theme-link">Continuous Discovery Habits</span>_ and am keen to start applying the opportunity solution tree framework, and I have also ordered _<span class="theme-link">Escaping the Build Trap</span>_ by Melissa Perri after seeing it recommended by multiple sources. I dug into the <span class="theme-link">RICE</span> Prioritisation Framework to sharpen my understanding of scoring models before adding reference notes to my <a href="/projects/bangsluke-documentation" class="theme-link">Documentation Site</a>.

Networking has been encouraging too. I've reached out to an old contact from my time at <span class="theme-link">Opus 2</span>, about having a chat around my move into product - and he's up for finding a date. I also had a really nice catch-up call with another colleague, where we talked through what we'd both been up to over the past few years. That conversation also touched on a potential <span class="theme-link">Data Analysis</span> opportunity at his company, which sounded more promising than I'd initially expected.

On the technical side, I did some further <span class="theme-link">SQL</span> practice - starting with ThoughtSpot's intermediate tutorials before getting frustrated with the interactivity and switching to Data Lemur's guide, which pointed me to SQLBolt - a much better experience. I also reviewed my <a href="/projects/dorkinians-website-v3" class="theme-link">Dorkinians Website V3</a> and mapped out a full test suite covering unit, integration, and <span class="theme-link">E2E</span> tests using <span class="theme-link">Jest</span>, <span class="theme-link">Testing Library</span>, and <span class="theme-link">Playwright</span>. Separately, I discovered the site had crashed, so I fixed it and set up <span class="theme-link">UptimeRobot</span> to send me alerts if the frontend or backend goes down - a small but useful operational improvement.

## Other Updates

I continued doing some voluntary work at <span class="theme-link">Wyburns Primary School</span>, helping automate admin processes so that teachers can spend more time on what actually matters - teaching. It's a different context from my usual projects, but the core principle is the same: identify repetitive manual work and remove it.
