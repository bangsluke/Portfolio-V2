After a break for Easter, I'm back at it, progressing with interviews towards a career in product.

## Progress So Far

As my wife was off work for Easter, I dialled back my day to day schedule of learning and applying

The headline is that I've progressed through three separate interviews for product-focused roles. I won't go into detail on specifics, but the process itself has been valuable - each conversation sharpens how I articulate my experience and helps me understand what different organisations are actually looking for in these roles.

To support that, I spent time writing up more <span class="theme-link">STAR</span> examples for common interview questions. Having these prepared and practised makes a noticeable difference.

I also had some really useful feedback on my CV from a former colleague who has built a successful career in <span class="theme-link">Product</span>. Off the back of that, I extended the CV to a two-page layout to better detail my career experience and give each role the space it deserves. The single-page version was too compressed to tell the full story. I updated my <a href="/projects/job-document-creator-tool" class="theme-link">Job Document Creator Tool</a> at the same time to reflect the new format.

On the reading side, I've continued working through _<span class="theme-link">Continuous Discovery Habits</span>_ - slower than the previous weeks given the Easter break, but still progressing.

## Technical Updates

On the <a href="/projects/dorkinians-website-v3" class="theme-link">Dorkinians Website V3</a>, I fixed a recurring seeding issue in the database and updated the underlying logic to make it more robust going forward - the kind of invisible maintenance work that prevents bigger headaches later. I also rolled out new features using feature flags for the first time, which was a great learning experience in controlled releases and managing risk for a live user base. The site has also seen a surge in user traffic recently, which meant upgrading my <span class="theme-link">Umami</span> analytics account to handle the increased volume.

## Other Updates

Not much else to report this time around - the Easter break was a welcome reset. Sometimes the most productive thing you can do is step back for a bit.
